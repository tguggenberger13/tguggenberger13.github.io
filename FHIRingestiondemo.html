FHIR Ingestion

Overview

When it was introduced to me, FHIR was explained as being "Lego for healthcare." The analogy being that each “resource” — claims, medications, lab results, etc. — can snap neatly together with the next, forming whatever structure may needed without the usual data chaos. The beauty, of course, is in the modularity where resources can be custom assembled to build something bigger: be that a patient record, clinical dashboard or even a full-blown application.

Much more (and higher quality) information can be found elsewhere online, with a few good starting points being:  FHIR,  HL7,  Epic, and Azure.

Shown below is a short and simple demonstration on ingesting and combining FHIR resources - in this case, we join Patient and Condition data, while also reviewing Claims and Providers. This is done using freely available synthetic data courtesy of Synthea.
