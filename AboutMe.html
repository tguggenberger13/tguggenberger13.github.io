About

My career sits at the intersection of healthcare, strategy, and hands-on building. With experience across consulting and data engineering, I'm drawn to ambiguous problems, building data infrastructure at scale, and designing solutions that make clinical and operational decisions easier. I find myself most energized when I'm flexing between engineering and consultative roles—whether that's interfacing with clinicians, analysts, and stakeholders to translate technical requirements into shared decisions, or architecting pipelines that turn messy data into something actually useful. I enjoy the challenges of a diverse workload and have experience across a number of domains including quality measurement and reporting, revenue cycle management, provider compensation and productivity, population health, clinical operations, and health information interoperability.

And finally, in no particular order, below are a few ideas that I've been turning over—some I've explored in various degrees of depth, others still sitting in the mental parking lot:

  • Leveraging Digital Twins for patient-specific N-of-1 trials and treatment optimization
  • Integrating IoT and SDOH into Digital Twin models
  • Building agentic AI workflows that actively surveil EHR data for anomalies, deterioration signals, and missed diagnoses
  • Using data to build synthetic control groups at scale
  • Building streaming ETL pipelines for continuous glucose monitoring data to drive personlized diabetes management
  • Mining unstructured clinical data to surface care gaps and intervention opportunities
  • Using IoT and remote patient monitoring to enhance Hospital-at-Home programs

    • If any of this resonates or you'd like to chat further, please don't hesitate to reach out. I love connecting with people working on similar challenges and always happy to share notes and exchange ideas.
