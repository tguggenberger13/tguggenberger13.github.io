1 — The Problem

Every Grant Expense Is a Compliance Judgment
That Almost Nobody Is Checking

When an AP clerk at an FQHC codes an invoice to a federal grant, they're making an implicit compliance decision: "This cost is allowable under 2 CFR 200 and our grant terms." In reality, most organizations aren't checking this until month-end review — or worse, during the annual Single Audit. By then, questioned costs, repayments, and audit findings are already baked in. The knowledge exists (2 CFR 200 Subpart E is publicly available), but nobody checks it at the point of transaction.

Manual Policy Lookups

Finance staff don't memorize 55+ cost categories in 2 CFR 200. They rely on experience, ask colleagues, or hope for the best. The result: unallowable costs slip through routinely.

No Real-Time Validation

ERPs don't enforce grant compliance rules. A $500 entertainment expense coded to HRSA posts immediately, even though it's explicitly unallowable. The error surfaces months later during audit prep.

Allocation Cap Overruns

Indirect cost rate caps (e.g., 15% of direct costs) are tracked manually in spreadsheets. By the time finance realizes they've exceeded the cap, correcting entries are painful and visible to auditors.

Audit Findings Are Costly

Questioned costs trigger repayments, corrective action plans, and reputational damage. A single finding can jeopardize future funding. Prevention is worth 10x remediation.

2 — The Solution

A Validator, Not a Gatekeeper

The Grant Compliance Validator runs in the background — checking every transaction coded to a federal grant against 2 CFR 200 rules and grant-specific terms. It doesn't block staff from entering data; instead, it flags issues in a leadership dashboard where they can be reviewed and corrected before month-end. Think of it as spell-check for grant compliance: non-intrusive, always running, and impossible to ignore once it's caught a mistake.

STEP 1

Transactions Post Normally

AP staff enter bills and journal entries in NetSuite or Intacct with zero interruption. The validator observes silently.

STEP 2

Validator Runs Nightly

All transactions coded to federal grants are checked against the rules engine: allowable categories, allocation caps, documentation requirements.

STEP 3

Issues Surface in Dashboard

CFO or grants manager sees flagged items: high-priority violations, missing docs, or warnings. Each includes the specific regulation reference and suggested action.

STEP 4

Corrections Happen Pre-Audit

Leadership recodes entries, attaches documentation, or marks exceptions — all before the auditor sees anything. Full audit trail is preserved.

4 — Technology

Lightweight, Deterministic, Low-Maintenance

The validator is not AI-powered or algorithmically complex — it's a rules engine with structured lookups. The 2 CFR 200 regulation changes infrequently (last major update: 2024), and grant-specific terms are uploaded by customers during onboarding. Once the core engine is built, maintenance is minimal.

BACKEND API

Modern server-side framework with relational database for rules storage, audit logs, and customer configuration. Scheduled job processing for nightly validation sweeps.

RULES ENGINE

Deterministic logic engine with structured rule definitions — simple if/then validation, no ML required. Rules update infrequently as regulations change.

ERP INTEGRATION

API connections to Sage Intacct and NetSuite for reading GL transactions and posting corrective journal entries. OAuth-based authentication.

DASHBOARD FRONTEND

Modern web framework with component library for clean, responsive UI. Charts and visualization for compliance scoring and trend analysis.

BROWSER EXTENSION (OPTIONAL)

Lightweight JavaScript extension for real-time hints as staff enter data — non-blocking, educational UI overlay.

INFRASTRUCTURE

Cloud hosting with managed database, error monitoring, and uptime tracking. Standard SaaS stack with emphasis on reliability over complexity.

Build estimate: 8–12 weeks for MVP with one full-stack engineer. The product is focused enough that a small, experienced team can ship v1.0 quickly and iterate based on design partner feedback.
